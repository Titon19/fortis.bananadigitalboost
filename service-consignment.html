The Concept

Inventory That Pays for Itself.

Consignment inventory is a supply arrangement where Fortis holds the stock at or near your facility, and you only pay for what you actually consume. There is no upfront capital commitment, no risk of overstock, and no gap in supply availability.

For plant operations with ongoing MRO, safety, or consumable needs, this model eliminates the friction of repeat purchase orders while ensuring critical items are always available when needed — at the exact moment your operations require them.

"Fortis holds the stock. You consume what you need. You pay for what you use. Nothing more."

The Model

Three Steps, Zero Supply Gaps.

1
Fortis Stocks

We identify your recurring product needs and establish an agreed consignment stock level at or near your facility.

2
You Consume

Your team accesses stock when required — no purchase orders, no delays, no minimum order quantities.

3
You Pay

At agreed intervals, you pay only for items consumed. We replenish automatically to maintain your agreed stock level.

Why It Works

The Business Case for Consignment

Cash Flow
Zero Capital Tied in Inventory

Your working capital stays in your business, not sitting on a shelf. There is no upfront investment, no minimum purchase commitment, and no risk of carrying obsolete stock.

Operations
Supply Available When You Need It

Critical parts and consumables are always on hand. No more emergency sourcing, no production stoppages due to missing components, and no reactive purchasing at premium prices.

Efficiency
Fewer Purchase Orders, Less Admin

The repeat cycle of raising purchase orders, approval workflows, and receipting is replaced by a single periodic invoice for actual consumption. Less paperwork, less cost.

Risk
No Overstock, No Write-Offs

Fortis manages the risk of stock sitting unused. If your consumption patterns change, we adjust the consignment level — you are never stuck with stock you don't need.

Best Fit

Operations That Benefit Most

01
Manufacturing Plants
Facilities with high-frequency MRO consumption that need parts available immediately to avoid production downtime.
02
Oil & Gas Operations
Remote or offshore operations where supply chain delays are costly and always-available stock is operationally critical.
03
Construction & Engineering
Projects with ongoing consumable needs across safety, tools, and maintenance categories — without the overhead of managing a procurement team.
04
Multi-Site Operations
Companies managing multiple facilities can consolidate consignment stock management under one partner for consistent availability and pricing.
